The Mom Test

"The Mom Test" by Rob Fitzpatrick is an insightful and invaluable guide for entrepreneurs, product managers, and anyone involved in the early stages of product development. With a refreshingly practical approach, this book challenges the traditional methods of customer interviews and offers a framework to gather honest and reliable feedback.
One of the book's strengths lies in its emphasis on validating assumptions and avoiding the temptation to seek validation for our own ideas. Fitzpatrick guides readers on how to extract honest feedback from potential customers without falling into the trap of confirmation bias.

Hooked

"Hooked: How to Build Habit-Forming Products" by Nir Eyal is a must-read book for anyone who is involved in creating or developing products. Eyal presents a framework for building habit-forming products that are designed to keep users engaged and coming back for more.
The book is divided into four parts that cover the four steps of the Hook Model: trigger, action, variable reward, and investment. Eyal provides numerous real-life examples and case studies throughout the book to illustrate the concepts and to help readers understand how they can be applied to their own products.

Thinking in Bets

Thinking in Bets is probably the best book for decision making that I have read. Poker champion turned decision strategist and speaker Anne Duke draws on examples from business, sports, politics, and (of course) poker to share tools anyone can use to embrace uncertainty and make better decisions.
By shifting your thinking from a need for certainty to a goal of accurately assessing what you know and what you don't, you'll be less vulnerable to reactive emotions, knee-jerk biases, and destructive habits in your decision making. You'll become more confident, calm, compassionate and successful in the long run.
